Director

Cristóbal Ruiz is an emerging documentary filmmaker and photographer. His work, both alluring and emotive, focuses on stories that inspire change in our surrounding communities. Cristóbal was born and raised in Chile and today he is based in between Canada, Chile and Germany. (Currently residing in Berlin). Cristóbal's work has taken him from South America to Europe and East Africa documenting stories of hope. He is the founder of the production company "Pachamama", which focuses on producing storytelling films that will inspire change in our surrounding communities. Cristóbal is the Director, producer and photographer of Añay Kachi: The Salt Workers of the Peruvian Andes. He is also an ambassador for @peoplesplanetproject. An organization that empowers indigenous communities from rainforests around the world through the power of filmmaking and photography as tools in the legal battle to defend their lands from deforestation. More of Cristóbal’s work at www.PachamamFilm.com

Tom is a director and DOP based out of England. His work focuses on high impact filmmaking with purpose-packed storytelling. Some of Tom’s projects have taken him to remote locations from Iceland, Indonesia and Peru, all the way to the remote corners of Antarctica documenting some of the harshest locations in the planet. Tom’s work, directing or as a DOP is of pure excellence in all conditions. Tom is also the founder of Redwood Film a world class that focuses on remote locations, outdoors and environmental films and a Co-Founder of Pachamama Film. Tom can be mostly found surfing in his backyard in Cornwall, England or travelling tVhe world looking for the next story. Tom is the Co-Director, producer and Director of Photography of Añay Kachi: The Salt Workers of the Peruvian Andes. More of tom’s work can be seen at www.RedwoodFilm.com

Co-Director

Toto Thot is a cinematographer, director and sound recordist based out of Chile. Toto is a hybrid mixing storytelling, sound and forward thinking filmmaking. A powerful storyteller, with a vision that stays truthful to the story he is trying to tell. Toto’s work is nothing but rawness in a cinematic form. When on the field and in post-production, Toto makes sure that the story is meaningful and realistic to his subjects and keeps production with their feet on the ground, not only directing but also developing real relationships with the characters he is documenting. Today, Toto can be found in the coast of Chile, recording sounds of the beautiful landscapes he transits, writing his next project and filming subjects that represent change. Toto is the Co-Director, and sound recordist of Añay Kachi: The Salt Workers of the Peruvian Andes. More work at https://www.instagram.com/totofuentes91
